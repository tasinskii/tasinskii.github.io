    ✱ Image Denoising with Monte Carlo Markov Chain Gibbs Sampling

    Implemented in Python as part of a course in probabilistic graphical models. Images are represented by the Ising Model, originally used in statistical mechanics. To learn the relationships between pixel positions and a noisy image, a random walk is taken along the markov chain of image state possibilities, with more probabilistically likely samples used to update the graphical model's parameters. Some results: MNIST, Cat.

    + Python, NumPy, Markov Random Fields, MCMC sampling

    ✱ Microscopy Sample Analysis with a Convolutional Neural Network -- Link

    My first two summers at Northwestern, I worked in the VPD Lab, a materials science research group focused on electron microscopy. As part of my work, I created a GUI application to process and analyze 4D Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy (4D STEM) data. This data can offer a wealth of information about a sample, so as part of my GUI tool, I incorporated a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to determine the thickness of a sample. Additionally, users can use the tool to develop processed images based on a chosen sub-section of the total data.

    + Python, OpenCV, Threading, PyQt6, NumPy, matplotlib, Keras

    ✱ Animated WebGL Scene with Textures and Phong Lighting -- Link

    For my Computer Graphics class, our final project was to incorporate a Phong lighting model and textures into a scene of our choosing. The scene shows a low-poly version of myself pouring tea for a low-poly version of my girlfriend, Maya. I modeled the figures and textures in Blender. The project first parses the saved Blender .obj files, then for every frame draws the models, textures, and lighting to the screen, and applies transformations to the models to animate the figures. The user can manipulate the position of a camera, as well as the position of the light source.

    + WebGL, Javascript, Blender
